The Great Gatsby

Description

F. Scott Fitzgerald's iconic novel, The Great Gatsby, was published in 1925. Inspired by his own experiences with the wealthy and glamorous socialites of the 1920s, Fitzgerald crafted a tale of love, loss, and the American Dream. After a period of creative inspiration on the French Riviera, he completed a draft of the novel and, with the guidance of his editor,Maxwell Perkins , refined the manuscript. Despite uncertainty about the title, Fitzgerald ultimately settled on The Great Gatsby, a name that would become synonymous with the Jazz Age.

Her Land, Her Love

Description

This sweeping historical novel chronicles the struggles of a Navajo family during the Long Walk. With a focus on love, relationships, and community, the story centers on Ninaanibaa' and Hashke Yil Naabaah, a courageous couple whose daughters are tragically kidnapped. As they embark on a perilous journey to find their lost children, the novel blends historical accuracy with a captivating love story, offering a poignant exploration of Navajo culture and resilience.
